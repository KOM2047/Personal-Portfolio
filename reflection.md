## 1. What I Learned

Throughout this bootcamp, my most significant realization was that **data does not live in a vacuum**. While learning the individual components of the stack—**Cloud, AI, DevOps, and Data**—I discovered that the true value lies in the **intersection of these fields**.

Coming from a **Cybersecurity background**, I initially viewed these technologies as separate attack vectors to be secured. However, during the bootcamp, I learned to view them as **building blocks for a robust ecosystem**. Key insights included:

- **Cloud (AWS):** Not just hosting; it is the backbone of scalable architecture.  
- **DevOps:** Not just about speed; it is about the reliability and integrity of the data pipeline.  
- **Data Engineering:** The engine that drives business value, requiring the rigor of software engineering practices.  

---

## 2. Technical Strengths

I have identified my core competency as **DataSecOps**—the practice of building secure, automated data infrastructures. My strongest areas are:

- **Cloud Architecture (AWS):** Designing serverless and containerized solutions (Lambda, S3, Docker) that are secure by design.  
- **Advanced SQL & Warehousing:** Structuring complex schemas that maintain data integrity while optimizing for analytical performance.  
- **Python Automation:** Writing robust extraction and transformation scripts that replace manual, error-prone processes.  
- **AI Integration:** Leveraging my Azure AI Engineer certification to embed intelligence into data workflows.

## 3. Capstone Integration

My Capstone project was the culmination of these skills. It demonstrated my ability to:
